A normal day inside a founder-dependent agency

Your team has a working day.
You have two.

  1. 09:10
    Morning: the approval queue opens

    Campaigns, copy and client replies wait for the judgement no one else is authorised to make.

  2. 12:40
    Midday: “quick questions” consume the plan

    Your sales work gets displaced by decisions that should already have an owner and a threshold.

  3. 17:25
    Evening: client risk reaches you

    A missed handoff or quality issue becomes your rescue because the system detects exceptions too late.

  4. 21:15
    Late: you finally do the founder’s job

    Growth, strategy and partnerships begin after the operating day has already taken its share.

12 — Before you book

The questions that should be answered before a call.

Does this replace an operations manager?

No. VBOG installs the authority, source of truth, capacity model and operating cadence that make an operations manager effective. We can help appoint or supervise the internal operator after the Sprint.

What does “5–10 clients” actually mean?

It means equivalent Standard Client Capacity Units, defined from your service mix, delivery hours, role mix and review burden. A light account might be 0.5 units; a complex multi-channel account may be 2.5. The promise is never based on raw client count alone.

What if our main constraint is sales?

Then this is the wrong intervention. VBOG creates delivery capacity; it does not manufacture demand. The Sprint requires proven demand, a visible pipeline or strong founder-led sales.

What do you need from me during the 90 days?

Timely access to client, delivery, team and financial data; participation in required sessions; a nominated internal operator; willingness to change roles and workflows; and respect for delegated authority inside agreed boundaries.

What happens after the Sprint?

Your internal operator owns the layer. If useful, VBOG can continue as an external COO-level supervisor—reviewing capacity, coaching exceptions and protecting the operating system from regression.
