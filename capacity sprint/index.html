11 — Before you apply

Sensible questions from serious founders.

Does this replace an operations manager?

No. A strong operations manager may be exactly what you need. VBOG installs the authority, source of truth, capacity model and operating cadence that make that person effective. We can help appoint or supervise the operator after the Sprint.

What does “5–10 clients” actually mean?

It means equivalent Standard Client Capacity Units, defined from your service mix, delivery hours, role mix and review burden. A light reporting account might be 0.5 units; a complex multi-channel account may be 2.5. The promise is never based on raw client count alone.

What if our main constraint is sales?

Then this is the wrong intervention. VBOG creates delivery capacity; it does not manufacture demand. The Sprint requires proven demand, a visible pipeline or strong founder-led sales.

Will you migrate our project-management tools?

Only if a separate business case makes it necessary. We use your existing tools wherever possible and build the minimum system required for good decisions. Automating an unclear process only scales confusion.

What do you need from me during the 90 days?

Timely access to client, delivery, team and financial data; participation in required sessions; a nominated internal operator; willingness to change roles and workflows; and respect for delegated authority inside agreed boundaries.

What happens after the Sprint?

Your internal operator owns the layer. If useful, VBOG can continue as an external COO-level supervisor—maintaining the scorecard, reviewing capacity, coaching exceptions and protecting the operating system from regression.
