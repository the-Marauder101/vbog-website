Step 2: Awaiting Response or Documents
⏰
The customer has not replied
If there is no response after 1 to 2 business days, send a gentle and polite follow-up. Keep the tone warm and patient. Do not send more than two follow-up messages before checking with your manager.
📂
The customer says they do not have the documents
Do not make the customer feel at fault. Acknowledge the situation, reassure them and ask them to share whatever they are able to. Let them know that even partial information is helpful.
Step 3: Documents Received, Log and Confirm
📥
All required documents have been received
Complete both actions below. Log the case internally first, then send the customer a confirmation so they know their complaint has been received and is being processed.
Internal Action
Log the complaint in the system, assign a reference number and notify the technical team. Save all documents received.
  • ✅Reference number assigned
  • ✅Documents saved and filed
  • ✅Technical team notified
Customer Action
Send the customer a warm confirmation. Include their reference number and let them know when to expect an update.
Step 4: Outcome from Technical Review
Outcome A: Claim Approved
The issue is covered under warranty. Proceed with a free site visit.
The technical team has confirmed the complaint falls within the warranty coverage and the warranty is still active. Communicate the approval and arrange the visit.
  • 📩Inform the customer their claim has been approved
  • 📅Coordinate a convenient time for the technical site visit
  • 👤Confirm the on-site contact person is available on the day
  • 🔑Make sure site access is arranged in advance
Outcome B: Not Covered by Warranty
The issue falls outside warranty scope. A call-out fee of 160 AED applies.
The warranty does not cover this complaint. This may be due to expiry, misuse, physical damage or another exclusion. A paid site visit is the next option. The customer must agree before any visit is booked.
Call-Out Fee: 160 AED (payable on the day of the visit)
  • 📩Inform the customer politely and clearly state the reason
  • 💰Explain the 160 AED call-out fee and what it covers
  • ✅Wait for the customer to confirm before booking anything
  • 📋See Step 5 below for how to handle their response
Other Situations That May Come Up
Outcome C: More Information Needed
The technical team cannot assess the case yet.
This may be because photos are unclear, a document is missing or the issue description is not detailed enough. Go back to the customer with a specific request. Do not send a generic message. Be clear about exactly what is needed.
  • 📋Check with the technical team on what is missing
  • 📝Note the specific gap before reaching out
  • 📩Send a polite message requesting only the missing item
Outcome D: Not an Atlantic Product
The unit is not an Atlantic Water Heater.
This can happen when a customer contacts the wrong company or is unsure of their brand. Handle this with full professionalism and patience. Do not dismiss the customer. Acknowledge their situation and redirect them politely.
  • 🔍Confirm the product is not an Atlantic unit before responding
  • 📩Politely inform the customer and suggest they contact the correct brand
  • 📋Log the interaction in the system for records
Outcome E: Emergency or Safety Concern
A potential hazard has been reported. Do not follow the standard process.
If the customer reports flooding, gas smell, electrical sparks, fire, or any other safety concern, this must be treated as an emergency. Reassure the customer immediately and escalate without delay.
1Tell the customer to stay calm and keep a safe distance from the unit
2If there is a gas smell or fire risk, advise them to leave the area and call emergency services (999)
3Call your manager or the technical team immediately. Do not handle this alone.
4Log the incident with a timestamp as soon as possible
The steps below apply only when the outcome is Outcome B (Not Covered, 160 AED call-out)
Step 5: Customer Response to the 160 AED Call-Out Fee
✅
The customer agrees to the 160 AED call-out fee
Once the customer confirms they are happy to proceed, book the site visit. The 160 AED fee is collected on the day of the visit by the technician. Do not book without explicit confirmation from the customer.
  • 📅Coordinate a visit date and time with the technical team
  • 👤Confirm the on-site contact and access arrangements
  • 💰Remind the customer the fee is 160 AED, payable on the day
  • 📋Log the booking in the system with the reference number
🔄
The customer declines or needs more time to decide
Respect the customer's decision. Do not pressure them. Keep the door open politely and make sure they know they can come back to us at any time. Log the outcome in the system and mark the case as pending the customer's decision.
  • 📋Update the case status in the system (pending customer decision)
  • 📩Send a polite close-out message that leaves the door open
  • 📅Add a reminder to follow up after 5 to 7 business days if no response
