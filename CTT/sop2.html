§ 1

Overview & How To Use This Document

This playbook standardizes every customer-facing process for Atlantic Water Heaters so that any team member handles them the same way, every time — regardless of who picks up the inquiry.

Three customer types / pathways run through these SOPs:

  • Retail Individual — an end consumer who bought (or owns) a unit directly.
  • Sadara / Contractor / Project Account — handled with a premade, account-level communication rather than the retail intake flow. Warranty here is often governed by a Take Over Certificate (TOC) rather than a reseller bill.
  • Prospect — someone who hasn't purchased yet (Sales Inquiry) or needs a part (Spare Parts Inquiry).
Each SOP follows the same structure: Purpose → Process Flow → Ready-to-use Templates → Turnaround Targets. Everything in a blue template box is copy-paste ready — just fill in the bracketed [placeholders]. Sections flagged in amber are drafted as a sensible default and should be confirmed or edited by you before the team starts using them.
Golden rules that apply to every SOP
  • Log every enquiry in Airtable — warranty complaint, spare part, sales, anything. Use the single Complaint Status field, and put the details in Intake: Brief Complaint. If it's not logged, it didn't happen.
  • Assign a Job Number internally — but never quote it to the customer. It's our job to know which client maps to which Job Number.
  • Never make a financial decision (replacement, PO, chargeable visit, discount) without the Manager's validation.
  • PO costs are strictly confidential — a customer never learns what a replacement or PO cost us.
  • The customer should never have to chase us — proactively update them, and never leave a complaint unattended.

SOP-01

Water Heater Complaint Intake

Purpose

To make sure every complaint — from a retail individual or a Sadara/project account — is acknowledged fast, correctly assessed for warranty, resolved at the desk where possible, and only sent to a technician when it genuinely needs one.

Scope

Applies to all inbound complaints about a malfunctioning, damaged, or defective Atlantic water heater, received via phone, email, WhatsApp, website form, or walk-in.

Roles (After-Sales team)

  • Front Desk Coordinator — first response, all customer communication, collects information, logs and updates Airtable, runs follow-ups.
  • Manager — warranty validation, initial escalation, discount/quotation approvals, dispatch coordination, writes site reports.
  • Technical Sales Manager — final escalations, key-account complaints (come to him directly, he routes to the front desk), on-site technical coordination, approves the final site report.
  • Technician — on-site diagnosis and repair.
Fast pathIf the complaint is a leak, don't start with the checklist. Jump to the Leakage Fast-Path below — get videos + the bill/TOC, rule out a Teflon-tape fix, and only then treat it as a warranty replacement.

Process Flow

1
Log & Acknowledge

Log the enquiry in Airtable: date, channel, customer name, contact number, the details in Intake: Brief Complaint, and set Complaint Status. Assign a Job Number for internal tracking (never shared with the customer).

Acknowledge the customer so they know someone is on it — target within 2 business hours. Keep it to plain communication; no Job Number in the message.

If the complaint is a leak → go to the Leakage Fast-Path.

Front Desk Coordinator
2
Collect Basic Information

We collect what we need to assess warranty — not a full "case file". Sadara/contractor/project → send the official intake email (Template SD-1) with the Checklist & Declaration form. Retail → request the basics (Template RT-1).

Ask for: photos/video of the fault, photo of the model + serial number label, and the reseller bill or the project's TOC (see Bill vs TOC below). For Sadara: also the site pin + access + site contact.

Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Awaiting Customer Documents'

Front Desk Coordinator
3
Confirm Receipt & Chase Gaps

Reply to confirm you received whatever came in — even if incomplete. If something's missing, send Template MD-1 listing only the missing items.

Follow the 4-day closure SLA (Step 8) if the customer goes quiet.

Front Desk Coordinator
4
Validate Warranty

Manager checks warranty against the bill (dated, lists the units — verify the specific unit) or, for a project, the TOC (the TOC date governs and covers all units in the project). Periods: 2 years electrical, 5 years body.

Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Warranty Verified' or 'Out of Warranty', with the reason.

Manager
5
Try to Resolve at the Desk First

Before dispatching anyone, attempt a remote fix: guidance on settings/heating time, a Teflon-tape re-seal for a minor connection leak, thermostat/usage checks (see SOP-04 Track B pre-checks).

Resolved remotely → confirm with the customer and close. Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Resolved — Remote'.

Front Desk Coordinator / Manager
6
Under Warranty & Can't Desk-Resolve → Dispatch

Verify the documents are complete, then hand to the Manager to schedule a technician (→ SOP-04). Over-the-phone troubleshooting can continue in the interim.

Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Under Warranty — Pending Site Visit'

Manager → Technician
7
Out of Warranty → Inform Customer

Send Template OW-1 with the specific reason and the AED 160 chargeable-visit option. If the issue is isolated to a part, offer to hand off to SOP-03 (Spare Parts) for a quote instead.

Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Out of Warranty — Customer Notified'

Front Desk Coordinator (Manager validates)
8
Follow Up & Close (4-day SLA)

If the customer goes quiet, follow up — a minimum of 4 unanswered follow-ups over 4 days. Before closing, send the close-out message ("Due to no response we're closing this for now — happy to reopen whenever you need support").

Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Closed — No Response'. Any case can be reopened later.

Front Desk Coordinator

Leakage Fast-Path

A leak doesn't need the full checklist to begin. Move fast — a real body leak means a replacement, and a connection leak is a two-minute fix.
L1
Get Video + Proof of Warranty

Skip the checklist. Ask for a clear video of the leak and the reseller bill, or the TOC if it's a project. This is enough to start.

Front Desk Coordinator
L2
Leak, or Just Teflon Tape?

From the video, decide: is it the body/tank leaking, or is it weeping at the inlet/outlet connection? If it's the connection, ask the customer (or their plumber) to re-apply Teflon tape on the threads (Template BL-2).

Front Desk Coordinator / Manager
L3
Teflon Fixed It → Wait 24h, Confirm, Close

If it was a connection leak and the tape resolved it, wait 24 hours, confirm with the customer that it's dry, and close the ticket. Update Airtable: Complaint Status = 'Resolved — Teflon'.

Front Desk Coordinator
L4
Real Body Leak & Under Warranty → Replacement

If it's a genuine body/tank leak and warranty checks out (bill or TOC), issue a replacement via the two-option process in SOP-05 Section 5 (our stock for Sadara, or a PO to a reseller). Supply-only — installation is not included.

Manager

Bill vs TOC — Which Date Governs Warranty

DocumentWhen it appliesHow we use it
Reseller BillRetail & most individual purchasesThe dated bill is the true warranty document and lists the units — verify the specific unit against it.
Take Over Certificate (TOC)Projects / handovers (the bill may be years older, e.g. 2020)The TOC date — when the project was handed over and heaters put to use — governs warranty. It covers all units in that project/apartment and does not list individual units.

SOP-04

Water Heater Site Visit — Verification & Service

Purpose

To make sure site visits are only dispatched when actually needed, that customers know upfront whether a charge could apply, and that technicians document findings clearly so the free-vs-chargeable decision is based on evidence.

Scope

Applies once a complaint can't be resolved at the desk and needs physical verification — covering both Body Leakage (tank) claims and Electrical (thermostat / heating element) claims.

Warranty Coverage at a Glance

ComponentWarranty Period
Electrical (thermostat, heating element)2 years
Body / Tank (leakage)5 years

Roles

  • Front Desk Coordinator — sends confirmation messages, applies the charge policy, keeps the customer updated.
  • Manager — validates warranty, coordinates dispatch, writes the site report.
  • Technical Sales Manager — on-site technical coordination for complex/key-account jobs; approves the final site report.
  • Technician — on-site diagnosis, documents findings, decides free vs. chargeable outcome with the Manager.

The Charge Policy (applies to both tracks)

Whenever a site visit is needed to verify a claim, send the Visit Confirmation (Template VC-1) first. Covered under warranty → no charge. Not covered → AED 160 per water heater unit (so a multi-unit site is AED 160 per unit; a single-unit site is a flat AED 160).

Dispatch runs to the SLA in Dispatch SLA & City Rules below — 48h for nearby cities, stacked for distant ones, but never left unattended.

Track A — Body Leakage

This is the on-site continuation of the Leakage Fast-Path — used when the leak can't be judged from video alone.

Request Evidence

Ask the customer for clear photos/video of the leak plus the bill/TOC before deciding anything else.

Front Desk Coordinator
A2

Front Desk Coordinator

Determine which outcome applies:

• Confirmed leak from the body/tank → covered defect. Go to Step A5 (free replacement).

• Leak at the inlet/outlet connection → fitting issue, not a warranty defect. Go to Step A5 (Teflon fix).

• Can't determine, or unit not installed → Go to Step A5 (pressure test).

If unclear, ask for better video before making a call — don't guess on something worth a free replacement.

Inlet/outlet leak → suggest re-applying Teflon tape (Template BL-2).

Front Desk Coordinator

Dispatch SLA & City Rules

Target a 48-hour dispatch-or-resolution SLA. When tickets are thin in a distant city, we stack visits so a technician isn't sent across the country for a single complaint — but a complaint is never left unattended. Worst case: 2–3 days with no new ticket; by day 4 we either give the customer a scheduled date or go the next day.
