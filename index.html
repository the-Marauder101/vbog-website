Objections Answered

Questions you're probably thinking.

How does profit share actually work — am I paying 20% of my revenue?
−
No. It's 20–30% of net profit only — not revenue, not gross margin. Net profit. If your business does $30K MRR with 15% net margins, your net profit is $4,500. My share is 20–30% of that $4,500 — between $900–$1,350/month. As the business scales and I transition out, my percentage reduces toward ~10%. The intent is that you make far more than you pay me, every single month.
Do I work with you personally, or get handed to a team?
+
Sprint: You work directly with me, 15–20 hours/week. No ops manager, no delegation — just me embedded in your business.

Full Partnership: I personally handle all strategic decisions — hiring, systems design, key implementations. A dedicated Ops Manager handles day-to-day execution under my oversight. You get a minimum of 2 direct check-ins per week with me.
What's the difference between Sprint and Partnership?
+
Sprint = Solve 1–3 specific problems. 8–12 weeks. Fixed fee. You keep control. Zero long-term commitment. It's a test drive — ideal if you want proof before a bigger commitment.

Partnership = I take over all operations, build your team, and scale the business to $100K+/month. 12-month minimum, profit-share aligned. The goal is your business running without me by the time I exit.

35% of Sprint clients convert to Full Partnership after seeing results — because they had confidence before committing.
Why profit share instead of a fixed monthly retainer?
+
Because my incentives need to be 100% aligned with your growth — not with billable hours. A flat retainer rewards showing up. Profit share rewards results.

As the business scales and the team becomes more self-sufficient, the percentage I take reduces — down to ~10% by exit. The intent is that you make significantly more than you pay me, every month of the engagement.
My main problem right now is sales. Can you still help?
+
Possibly — but only if the root cause is operational. If your delivery is inconsistent, your team can't handle more clients, or you're too stuck in execution to focus on sales — those are ops problems wearing a sales costume.

Here's the test: if I gave you 15 hours back per week by taking over your operations, would you know exactly what to do with those hours to grow?

If yes → we're probably a fit. If no → you need a sales or marketing person, not an ops person. I'll be honest on the discovery call either way.
What if the Sprint doesn't deliver results?
+
You keep everything we built — all systems, documentation, SOPs, and processes. The Sprint is structured to de-risk for both of us. That's why it exists.

If it doesn't work out, you walk away with working operational assets and no hard feelings. That's also why 35% of Sprint clients convert to Full Partnership voluntarily — they see results first, then commit.
How do I know if Sprint or Partnership is right for me?
+
Book a discovery call. After 30 minutes discussing your situation, I'll tell you which makes sense — or whether neither does right now. After the 7-day Operations Audit, it'll be completely clear. You never have to guess.
Do you require equity?
+
Sprint: No equity. Fixed fee only.

Full Partnership: Equity is optional (5–10%) for founders who want deeper alignment — but it's only ever discussed after Month 1 trial, never upfront. The majority of clients do profit share only with zero equity. The choice is entirely yours.
